The HBO Original drama series The Idol debuts Monday, 5 June, airing on HBO and available to stream on HBO GO.

The Idol is co-created by Sam Levinson (HBO’s Euphoria), Abel “The Weeknd” Tesfaye and Reza Fahim, and stars The Weeknd and Lily-Rose Depp. The series will premiere at this year’s Festival de Cannes.

After a nervous breakdown derailed Jocelyn’s (Depp) last tour, she’s determined to claim her rightful status as the greatest and sexiest pop star in America. Her passions are reignited by Tedros (The Weeknd), a nightclub impresario with a sordid past. Will her romantic awakening take her to glorious new heights or the deepest and darkest depths of her soul?

The show’s cast includes The Weeknd, Depp and Troye Sivan. Also starring Dan Levy, Da’Vine Joy Randolph, Eli Roth, Hari Nef, Jane Adams, Jennie Ruby Jane (Jennie of Blankpink), Mike Dean, Moses Sumney, Rachel Sennott, Ramsey, Suzanna Son and Hank Azaria.

The Idol is directed by Sam Levinson, and written by Sam Levinson, Abel “The Weeknd” Tesfaye and Reza Fahim.
