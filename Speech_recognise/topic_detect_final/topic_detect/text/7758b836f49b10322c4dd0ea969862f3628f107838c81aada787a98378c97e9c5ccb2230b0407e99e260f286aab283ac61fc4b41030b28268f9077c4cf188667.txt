A driver has been arrested for allegedly refusing to provide a roadside breath sample for police.

Kawartha Lakes Police say on Monday, April 17, at approximately 8:30 p.m. the Kawartha Lakes Police received a call from a concerned citizen about a driver parked on Settlers Road for over two hours.

An officer located the vehicle and while speaking with the driver allegedly observed signs of impairment. Police say the officer allegedly located alcohol in the vehicle that was readily available to the driver.

The officer allegedly determined the driver was impaired by alcohol and placed her under arrest.

As a result of the investigation, 44-year-old Lindsay women has been charged with failure or refusal to comply with demand.

The accused will appear at the Ontario Court of Justice located at 440 Kent Street West in Lindsay on May 25.
